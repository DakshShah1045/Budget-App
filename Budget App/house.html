Here are important financial ratios and guidelines to help you assess your readiness to buy a house:

1. House Price-to-Income Ratio - Recommended Ratio: 2.5 to 3 times your annual household income. - Explanation: Your house purchase price should ideally not exceed 2.5 to 3 times your annual income. For example, if your annual income is $80,000, you should aim for a home costing between $200,000 to $240,000.

2. Down Payment Percentage - Recommended Ratio: 20% of the home price. - Explanation: A 20% down payment is ideal as it reduces the loan amount and avoids private mortgage insurance (PMI). A higher down payment also results in lower monthly mortgage payments.

3. Housing Expense Ratio (Front-End Ratio) - Recommended Ratio: 28% or less of your gross monthly income. - Explanation: This ratio suggests that your monthly housing costs (including mortgage, property taxes, and insurance) should not exceed 28% of your gross income. If your gross monthly income is $5,000, your monthly housing costs should be below $1,400.

4. Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio - Recommended Ratio: 36% or less of your gross monthly income. - Explanation: This ratio includes all your monthly debts (mortgage, loans, credit cards). A DTI below 36% is ideal for mortgage approval. If your monthly income is $5,000, your total monthly debts, including housing, should be under $1,800.

5. Emergency Fund - Recommended Ratio: 3 to 6 months of living expenses. - Explanation: Before buying a house, it’s important to have an emergency fund that covers at least 3 to 6 months of your essential expenses. This acts as a financial cushion for unforeseen circumstances.

6. Maintenance and Property Tax Allocation - Recommended Ratio: 1% to 2% of the home's value annually. - Explanation: Allocate about 1% to 2% of the home’s value for annual maintenance and property taxes. If your home is worth $300,000, you should budget $3,000 to $6,000 per year.

7. Savings Rate for Down Payment - Recommended Ratio: 20% to 30% of monthly income directed towards saving for a down payment. - Explanation: It’s a good idea to save 20% to 30% of your monthly income for a down payment to reduce loan size and interest payments.

These ratios help balance your finances when buying a home and avoid overextending your budget.
